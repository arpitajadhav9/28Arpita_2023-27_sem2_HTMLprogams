Flowers

Blossom

1. Flower morphology consists of two main parts: the vegetative part, including non-reproductive structures like petals, and the reproductive or sexual parts.
2. A typical flower is composed of four structures attached to a short stalk called the receptacle.
3. Floral organs are arranged in a spiral fashion, forming four main whorls from the base upwards: calyx, corolla, androecium, and gynoecium.
4. The calyx and corolla together constitute the non-reproductive portion called the perianth; in some cases, they may not be distinguishable and are referred to as tepals.
